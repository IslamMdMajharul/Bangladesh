About the Country

An overview of history, society, and modern identity.

Bangladesh’s identity is closely connected to the Bengali language and cultural traditions. Its modern history includes the Language Movement and independence in 1971, shaping a strong national commitment to heritage and progress.

Today, Bangladesh is recognized for its textiles and manufacturing sectors, an expanding digital economy, and a young population contributing to innovation and growth.

Culture & Traditions

Festivals, arts, hospitality, and everyday life.

Cultural life features festivals such as Pohela Boishakh (Bengali New Year), traditional music, and crafts including weaving and pottery. Hospitality is a valued social norm, reflected in family-centered gatherings and shared meals.

From urban Dhaka to rural river communities, traditions remain dynamic—preserved while evolving with modern life.
